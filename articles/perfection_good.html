perfection vs good

Perfection is the enemy of good. This is an adage that has been echoed throughout time, so why is it so hard to avoid perfection anyways?

It always seems like — even though you know that having anything on a page, or an article, or just a minecraft base is best — I always fall into the trap of trying to perfect. I know you do too.

Kind of weird how that always ends up happening…

There’s ways around it, of course. There is the method of writing drunk and editing sober. Just get everything you are thinking onto a page, then edit from there. That method is a little exhausting at times though, and getting thoughts into words are sometimes difficult. I guess just writing them is the point though, it doesn’t really need to make sense. It’s taxing,

From there, it’s just constant revision, unless you end up liking the first revision. That’s very unlikely though.

You could also stop worrying, I guess. That’s also a solution.

On a similar note, there is also the 80-20 rule, where 20% of things gives 80% of the results. The last 20% is always the hardest. Maybe we need to settle for mediocrity. But that eventually leads to stagnation.

…

Anyways that’s the end. There was no real good way to end this one, and there aren’t really any better solutions I can offer. Maybe I come up with examples of good getting in the way of perfection. I may not. Who knows.

I’ll let you know if I ever find a solution.
