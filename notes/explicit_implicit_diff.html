explicit and implicit differentiation

First, some formulae…

\(y=e^{f(x)}\to y'=f'(x)e^{f(x)}\) \(y=\sin[f(x)]\to y'=\cos[f(x)]f'(x)\) \(y=\cos[f(x)]\to y'=-\sin[f(x)]f'(x)\) \(y=\tan[f(x)]\to y'=\frac{1}{\cos^2[f(x)]}f'(x)\) \(y=\cot[f(x)]\to y'=\frac{1}{\sin^2[f(x)]}f'(x)\) \(y=\ln[f(x)]\to y'=\frac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\)

explicit, implicit differentiation

Explict differention gets you something like:

\[y=x^3+2x^2+x+1\to y'=3x^2+4x+1\]

Implicit differentiation gets you something like

\[\begin{align} y&=x^2+3y^2-4e^y \\ y'&=2x+6y(y')-4e^{y}(y') \\ y'-6y(y')+4e^{y}(y')&=2x \\ y'(1-6y+4e^{y})&= \\ y'&=\frac{x}{1-6y+4e^{y}} \end{align}\]

You always patch it up with a y’ I guess.
