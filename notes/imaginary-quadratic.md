$$\begin{align}
ax^{2}+bx+c=0\text{ has solutions }p\text{ and }q \\
\iff \\
ax^{2}+bx+c=a(x-p)(x-q)
\end{align}$$

This also applies to imaginary numbers too! Complex solutions to polynomials comes in pairs, they are always conjugates of each other.
