circular motion

Rectilinear motion is motion across straight lines (rectangle linear?). Not all motion is rectilinear, and this complicates things. One of these is circular motion.

Think of circular motion as a bunch of little rectilinear motions one after another. There is always a force acting perpendicular to the motion in circular motion. This is the centripedal force.

\[F_{c}=\frac{mv^{2}}{r}\]
  • F is the centripedal force
  • m is the mass in kg
  • v is the speed
  • r is the radius of the path
\[a_{c}=\frac{v^{2}}{r}\]
  • a is the centripedal acceleration
\[v=\frac{2\pi r}{T}\]
  • This is the speed
